About Our School

Our History & Mission

Founded in 1975, St. Michael's High School has been at the forefront of providing quality education in Kenya. We are committed to nurturing well-rounded individuals who excel academically, socially, and spiritually.

Our mission is to provide a holistic education that empowers students to achieve their full potential and become responsible citizens who contribute positively to society.

With a student population of over 1,200 and a dedicated staff of 85 teachers, we maintain a conducive learning environment that fosters academic excellence and character development.

Overview & FAQs

School Overview

St. Michael's High School is a premier educational institution located in the heart of Kenya. We offer both the 8-4-4 curriculum and the new Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) to cater to the diverse needs of our students.

Our school is committed to providing a holistic education that balances academic excellence with character development, preparing students for success in their future endeavors.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the school hours?

School runs from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM from Monday to Friday. Extra-curricular activities may extend beyond these hours.

Does the school offer boarding facilities?

Yes, we have both day and boarding options for students. Our boarding facilities are modern and well-maintained.

What is the student-teacher ratio?

We maintain a ratio of 1 teacher for every 25 students to ensure personalized attention.

Our School History

Established in 1975

St. Michael's High School was founded in 1975 with the vision of providing quality education to the local community. What started as a small institution with just 50 students has grown into one of the most respected schools in the region.

Over the years, we have expanded our facilities, curriculum, and teaching staff to meet the evolving needs of education in Kenya.

Key Milestones

  • 1975: School established with 50 students and 5 teachers
  • 1985: Science laboratories constructed
  • 1995: Library and computer lab added
  • 2005: New administration block completed
  • 2015: Introduction of the Competency-Based Curriculum
  • 2020: Digital learning platform implemented

Service Character

At St. Michael's High School, we believe in instilling a strong sense of service and community responsibility in our students. We encourage them to become active citizens who contribute positively to society.

CBE Pathways Offered

Competency-Based Education

St. Michael's High School has fully embraced the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) to prepare students for the dynamic demands of the 21st century.

Pathways Available

  • Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM): For students interested in careers in sciences, technology, engineering and mathematics.
  • Social Sciences: For students interested in law, arts, humanities and social sciences.
  • Arts and Sports Science: For students with talents in creative arts, sports and related fields.
  • Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET): For students interested in technical skills and vocational training.

Our Approach

We focus on developing competencies rather than just content knowledge, ensuring our students are well-prepared for higher education and the world of work.

Admission Process

How to Apply

We welcome applications from students who demonstrate academic potential and a commitment to our school values. Our admission process is designed to identify students who will thrive in our learning environment.

Admission Requirements

  • Completed application form
  • Copy of birth certificate
  • Copy of KCPE results slip
  • Two recent passport-sized photographs
  • Previous school report cards (if applicable)

Application Timeline

Applications for Form One intake are accepted between January and March each year. Entrance exams are typically held in April, with results released in May.
